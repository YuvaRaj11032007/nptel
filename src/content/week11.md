## Summary

Week 11 provides a cohesive computational workflow focused exclusively on Prokaryotic genomes: taking raw bacterial samples, structuring their physical genome assembly, annotating the coding and non-coding landscape, and inferring larger evolutionary and metabolic networks.

The week opens with **Sequencing and Assembly**. For bacterial projects, Short-read (Illumina) data is often hybridized with Long-read (PacBio/Nanopore) data to close sequence gaps and finalize draft sequences into complete circular chromosomes. Tools like `SPAdes` organize these short strings via overlap graphs into overarching contigs. A structural analysis mapped against a reference determines precise elements like the Origin of Replication (*oriC*), the replication terminus, and inherent **GC-skew**, which naturally shifts on leaning versus lagging DNA framing.

With the DNA frame assembled, Lecture 2 transitions strictly into **Gene Annotation**. Bacterial genomes are compact, dense with operons, and importantly lack splice-junction introns, making prediction mostly a continuous-sequence exercise. Identifying *Open Reading Frames (ORFs)* is performed mostly via **Ab Initio algorithms** utilizing trained Hidden Markov Models (`Glimmer`) or Machine-Learning optimization (`Prodigal`) to locate hidden regulatory states like Ribosome Binding Sites and precise ATG/GTG start codons. Independent tools identify critical non-coding segments like `tRNAscan-SE` for structural tRNAs and `Barrnap` for 16S/23S ribosomal RNAs. Newly verified annotations are classified by functional taxonomy using databases like `eggNOG` (mapping to COG functional pools) and the Gene Ontology (GO) terms.

Finally, Lecture 3 integrates metabolic and evolutionary history. **Phylogenetics** mathematically maps the genetic distance between species utilizing substitution mutation rates mapped directly against ribosomal DNA like the 16S subunit. Divergence trees constructed through algorithms like Maximum Parsimony or Maximum Likelihood highlight macro-evolutionary ties. Lastly, to test the functional phenotype generated by these genomes, **Metabolomics** processes raw LC-MS and GC-MS mass-spectrometry spikes using packages like `MZmine` to identify all actively produced sub-molecules, which are searched through databases like the `Human Metabolome Database (HMDB)` to understand the living state of a microbiome network.

## Entities
* **SPAdes**: A multi-pipeline standard assembly toolkit optimized perfectly for generating structurally complete bacterial single-cell/multi-cell genomes.
* **Glimmer & Prodigal**: Premier computational annotation programs for microbial DNA. Both utilize non-homologous, mathematical learning algorithms (Interpolated Markov Models and ML respectively) to predict protein-coding ranges exclusively from the nucleotide patterns.
* **MZmine**: An open-source bioinformatic processing framework tailored precisely to convert uncalibrated raw mass-spectrometry files (LC-MS / GC-MS) into defined peak features for downstream identification as metabolic compounds.

## Concepts
* **GC-Skew**: A statistical measure utilized heavily in locating origins of replication in circular bacterial genomes. Since replication mechanics force distinct mutational biases, observing a sharp inversion in the (G-C)/(G+C) ratio often signals the `oriC` or terminus.
* **Ab Initio Prediction**: Gene finding strictly derived from mathematical probability (such as codon-usage bias or hexamer frequencies), unlike Evidence/Homology prediction mapping which must query the sample against physically known cDNA/Protein databases.
* **Maximum Parsimony vs Likelihood**: Core algorithmic paradigms in tree-building. Parsimony strives to compute the topological tree structure using the least number of total evolutionary mutations. Likelihood searches for the tree maximizing the statistical probability of observing the data given a specified model of substitution mutational evolution.
